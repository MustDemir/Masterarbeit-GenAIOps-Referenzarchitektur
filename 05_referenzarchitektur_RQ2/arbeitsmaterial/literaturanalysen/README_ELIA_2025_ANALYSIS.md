## Key Findings Summary

### Strengths
1. **Comprehensive Integration** – Successfully bridges EU AI Act, NIST RMF, TAI criteria, ISO standards
2. **Practical Operationalization** – Moves beyond abstract principles to concrete QG templates with metrics
3. **Lifecycle Holism** – Addresses all phases from conceptualization through decommissioning
4. **Flexibility by Design** – Generic Collection-QGs enable domain and use-case adaptation
5. **Transparent Risk Linkage** – Explicitly connects design decisions to identified risks
6. **Knowledge Evolution** – MQG4DK/MQG4A dual structure enables continuous improvement
7. **Standards Alignment** – Maps to multiple regulatory frameworks simultaneously

### Limitations
1. **Incomplete Implementation** – GitHub is template repository, not operational software
2. **Validation Gap** – No empirical demonstration on complete medical AI project
3. **Partial QMS Coverage** – Risk management emphasized; other aspects less developed
4. **Explainability Scope** – Detailed only for feature importance methods (SHAP/LIME)
5. **Organizational Readiness** – Framework assumes organizational capacity for coordination

### Relevance for RQ2 (Quality Gates)

**MQG4AI directly addresses RQ2 by:**
- Operationalizing quality gates from generic software engineering into AI-specific risk-based decision points
- Linking gates throughout complete AI lifecycle (not just development)
- Integrating regulatory compliance (EU AI Act) into gate definitions
- Providing concrete gate instantiation (Fidelity-Robustness Score)
- Establishing formal hierarchy (Collection-QGs → Leaf-QGs) enabling scaling
- Creating traceable information links connecting decisions to risks to artifacts to monitoring
- Proposing knowledge management framework for continuous gate evolution
