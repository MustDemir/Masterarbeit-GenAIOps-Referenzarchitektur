1. WHAT IS MQG4AI? (Question 1)
================================================================================

ACRONYM: Methodology based on Quality Gates for AI

DEFINITION:
MQG4AI is a generic, customizable framework for operationalizing Responsible AI 
(RAI) across the complete AI lifecycle. It bridges abstract RAI principles with 
concrete technical requirements through structured Information Management (IM).

CORE PURPOSE:
- Transform RAI guidelines (EU AI Act, NIST RMF, TAI criteria) into actionable 
  quality gate decision points
- Link every design decision to identified risks and risk controls
- Enable organizations to plan and monitor high-risk AI systems responsibly

KEY INNOVATION:
Decentralized knowledge management model (MQG4DK) feeding into project-specific 
implementations (MQG4A), enabling continuous evolution of RAI best practices 
while maintaining consistency across organizations.

3. WHAT QUALITY GATE STRUCTURE DOES IT PROPOSE? (Question 3)
================================================================================

TWO HIERARCHICAL QG TYPES:

Collection-QGs (Process Organization):
- Summarize and organize related sub-QGs
- Create vertical and horizontal interdependencies
- Build the AI lifecycle structure
- Enable scoring systems for specific dimensions
- Example: QG_Explanation encompasses configuration, evaluation, user interaction

Leaf-QGs (Design Decisions):
- Specify concrete design decisions with technical guidance
- Structured in three creation dimensions:
  1. Content (detailed explanation of decision)
  2. Method (implementation process)
  3. Representation (stakeholder-specific communication)
- Include four information layers:
  1. Input Information (dependencies)
  2. Output Information (downstream impacts)
  3. Evaluation Layer (limitations, drawbacks)
  4. Risk Management Layer (linked controls)

SIX LIFECYCLE PHASES (Collection-QG structure):
1. Conceptualization - Inception, requirements, business analysis
2. Data - Quality, preprocessing, transformation decisions
3. Development - Model creation, evaluation, refinement (with explanation stage)
4. Deployment - Real-world integration, pipeline adaptation
5. Maintenance - Continuous validation, drift detection, monitoring
6. Decommissioning - Model retirement, replacement decisions

NAMING CONVENTION:
QG_name_(view)
- name: design decision content
- view: applicable AI technique (e.g., SHAPLIME for SHAP/LIME explanations)
Tags: Enable intelligent search for use-case-specific QGs

================================================================================

EXPLICIT HIGH-RISK FOCUS:
- Title: "Towards Responsible High-risk AI"
- Mandatory integration of Article 9 (RMS) and Article 17 (QMS) from EU AI Act
- Medical domain primary use case (high-risk by AI Act definition)

RISK-BASED APPROACH:
1. Risk Identification - Linked to seven TAI criteria (Trustworthiness)
2. Risk Analysis - Structured per NIST RMF (DEFINE/ASSESS/TREAT/GOVERN)
3. Risk Control - Concrete mitigation strategies as leaf-QGs
4. Risk Monitoring - Post-market monitoring artifacts extracted from QGs
5. Residual Risk Evaluation - Iterative assessment across MQG4A-versions

SCALING FOR HIGH-RISK SYSTEMS:
- Mandatory inclusion of all 13 QMS aspects per Article 17
- Multiple MQG4A-versions for design decision comparison/validation
- Benefit-risk analysis when residual risk cannot be further reduced
- Traceability of all decisions for regulatory documentation

5. WHAT TRANSPARENCY/EXPLAINABILITY REQUIREMENTS MAP TO GATES? (Question 5)
================================================================================

EXPLAINABILITY AS TRANSPARENCY ILLUSTRATOR:

Two Major Explainability Risks Identified:
1. Incomprehensible Explanations to User
   - Risk: Methods not adapted to comprehension capacity
   - Control: Comprehensible interfaces, user interaction flows, notifications
   - Evaluation: Usability testing, user surveys (qualitative)

2. Unfaithful/Unreliable Explanations
   - Risk: Methods don't reflect true model reasoning
   - Control: Prefer ante-hoc models; validate via fidelity + robustness
   - Evaluation: Sanity checks, mathematical properties (quantitative)

THREE-STAGE EXPLANATION LIFECYCLE (Collection-QGs):

Configuration QG:
- Five decision factors:
  * Purpose: Why generate explanations (validate, assess preprocessing, inform, discover)
  * Applicability: Model-agnostic vs. model-specific
  * Scope: Global vs. local explanations
  * Result: Format of delivery (text, visualization, summary)
  * Stage: Post-hoc vs. ante-hoc generation

Evaluation QG:
- Usability (subjective): User comprehension, trust, satisfaction
- Quality (objective): Fidelity and robustness metrics

User Interaction QG:
- UI design
- User studies and usability testing
- Stakeholder-specific communication

CONCRETE METRIC: Fidelity-Robustness Score (0-1)
- Fidelity: Binary (0/1) - Do explanations pass sanity checks?
- Robustness: 0-1 - Do explanations remain consistent across data variations?
- Combined: Fidelity × Robustness
  * <0.8: questionable
  * ≥0.8: good
  * ≥0.9: pretty good

6. IS THERE A FORMAL DECISION LOGIC? (Question 6)
================================================================================

YES - NIST AI RMF Four-Step Integration:

1. DEFINE PHASE
   - Identify AI risks through collection-QGs
   - Consult AI Risk Ontology (AIRO) for system information
   - Map to seven TAI criteria

2. ASSESS PHASE
   - Evaluate identified risks for impact and likelihood
   - Compare design decisions through multiple MQG4A-versions
   - Assess residual risk after each mitigation attempt

3. TREAT PHASE
   - Implement risk controls as leaf-QG design decisions
   - Evaluate control effectiveness through evaluation layers
   - Iterate until residual risk acceptable (or benefit-risk justified)

4. GOVERN PHASE
   - Monitor through MQG4A-version iterations
   - Extract post-market monitoring artifacts
   - Document all design decision traceability

BIDIRECTIONAL INFORMATION FLOW:
- Upstream: System context/requirements → Design decisions
- Downstream: Design decisions → Risk implications → Monitoring artifacts

VERSIONING LOGIC (MQG4A-versions):
- Pre-selection: Initial design decision setup with requirements
- Intra-selection: Iterative refinement through testing/validation
- Post-selection: Final design decisions with performance validation

INTERDEPENDENCY TRACKING:
Each leaf-QG specifies:
- Input dependencies: Which other QGs must be available
- Output impacts: Which other stages affected
- Horizontal links: Same structural setting (model config affects explanations)

7. WHAT ARE THE GATE DELIVERABLES/ARTIFACTS? (Question 7)
================================================================================

INFORMATION MANAGEMENT ARTIFACTS PER QG:

Technical Specifications:
- Method implementation details
- Required assumptions and constraints
- Computational resource requirements
- Integration requirements with other lifecycle stages

Risk Documentation:
- Identified risks linked to design decision
- Applied control measures
- Residual risk assessment
- Acceptability justification

Post-Market Monitoring Inputs:
- Metrics to track in production
- Trigger thresholds for re-evaluation
- Data collection specifications
- Drift detection methods

Stakeholder-Specific Representations:
- AI Experts/Data Scientists: Full technical detail
- Domain Experts: Decision rationale and validation approach
- Regulators: Compliance mapping and documentation
- AI Users: Comprehensible explanation of system functioning

CONCRETE EXAMPLE: QG_FidelityRobustnessScore_(SHAPLIME)

Input Requirements:
- Trained model capable of LIME/SHAP explanation generation
- Test data representing intended domain
- Baseline performance metrics

Output Information:
- Fidelity-Robustness score (0-1)
- Trigger for re-evaluation when model retrained
- Sanity check results (model/data randomization tests)
- Robustness measurements (NDCG across data distributions)

Deliverable Format:
- Score of 0 = explanations not appropriate, cannot trust
- Score 0-0.8 = questionable, may need adjustment
- Score 0.8-0.9 = good quality
- Score >0.9 = pretty good quality

Usage:
- Integrated into post-market monitoring strategy
- Re-evaluated whenever model retrained
- Triggers adjustment of explanation method if score insufficient

10. RISK-BASED SCALING OF GATES? (Question 10)
================================================================================

RISK-BASED GATE SCALING MECHANISMS:

1. COLLECTION-QG HIERARCHY
   - High-level generalizable QGs at top level
   - Sub-levels grow with use-case-specificity
   - Each level can be evaluated against risks
   - Example: Explanation → Configuration → Purpose selection scales to domain

2. LEAF-QG PARAMETERIZATION
   Five-factor configuration for explanation gates:
   - Purpose-driven (why generate explanations)
   - Applicability-driven (model type considerations)
   - Scope-driven (global vs. local)
   - Result-driven (format adaptation)
   - Stage-driven (post-hoc vs. ante-hoc preference)
   
   Different risk profiles require different parameter combinations

3. MULTIPLE MQG4A-VERSIONS
   Pre-selection, Intra-selection, Post-selection phases enable:
   - Comparing baseline vs. modified design decisions
   - Testing different risk mitigation strategies
   - Validating design choices through iterative branches
   - Scaling effort based on risk level (more iterations for higher risk)

4. RESIDUAL RISK EVALUATION CYCLE
   - Risk identified → Control implemented → Residual risk assessed
   - If residual risk acceptable: Gate passes
   - If residual risk not acceptable: 
     * Further risk controls applied (loop back)
     * Or benefit-risk analysis conducted (high-risk special case)
     * Or gate fails (design decision rejected)

5. TAI CRITERIA MAPPING
   Risk scaling across seven trustworthiness dimensions:
   - High-risk systems: All seven TAI criteria must be addressed
   - Medium-risk: Subset of most critical criteria
   - Low-risk: Minimal TAI consideration
   
   MQG4AI configurable to scale TAI depth per risk level

6. MONITORING ARTIFACT SCALING
   Post-market monitoring requirements scale with risk:
   - High-risk: Frequent re-evaluation, strict thresholds (e.g., Fidelity-Robustness >0.9)
   - Medium-risk: Periodic re-evaluation, moderate thresholds (>0.8)
   - Low-risk: Infrequent review, relaxed thresholds

7. STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT SCALING
   MQG4AI stratifies stakeholders (active, consulting, passive):
   - High-risk systems: More active stakeholder involvement
   - Medium-risk: Key stakeholders actively involved
   - Low-risk: Minimal stakeholder coordination
   
   Information representation tailored to stakeholder type and risk level

8. INFORMATION MANAGEMENT DEPTH
   Input/Output/Interdependency complexity scales:
   - High-risk: Comprehensive interdependency mapping across all phases
   - Medium-risk: Key interdependencies mapped
   - Low-risk: Simplified dependency tracking
